Bioinformatics Data Skills

During my time as a bioinformatician at the UC Davis Genome Center and in the Dubcovsky and Ross-Ibarra labs, I found very few books taught the data skills I relied on most in my day-to-day bioinformatics work. Furthermore, texts didn't emphasize scientific computing best practices alongside teaching technical skills. Bioinformatics Data Skills (published by O'Reilly Media in July 2015) is my book that seeks to fill this role.

You can purchase Bioinformatics Data Skills directly from O'Reilly, from an independent bookstore like Powell's City of Books, or Amazon.com. You may wish to read the reviews on O'Reilly's site (4.8/5 stars, 13 reviews) or Goodreads (4.45/5 stars, 11 ratings) first.

Audience

Bioinformatics Data Skills is an intermediate-level book, aimed at readers with some experience with a scripting language like Python.

Most existing bioinformatics texts focus on algorithms and theories. Bioinformatics Data Skills takes a refreshingly practical approach by providing a comprehensive introduction to the techniques, tools, and best practices that foster reproducible research in genome informatics. Its hands on approach is perfect for graduate students, postdocs, professors, and hobbyists alike.
